Welcome


Plant It Herbs is a research and education facility, and specializes in growing nursery-propagated Chinese medicinal plants. The gardens contain perennials, annuals, vines and shrubs used in Chinese medicine for over 3000 years.

Currently, about 250 species of Chinese medicinal plants are growing on the grounds. In 2001 Plant It Herbs applied for Sustainable Agriculture, Research and Education (SARE) grant from the USDA. In July 2002, the grant was awarded and a Chinese medicinal plant study was begun to determine the medicinal quality of five important plants. While we now know many important Chinese medicinal plants grow well in southeastern Ohio, it’s not yet known if plants grown in Ohio soils will produce good quality medicine.

Research is being conducted on five Chinese medicinal plants grown on three separate sites in southeastern Ohio. Three medicinal mushrooms grown on five local hardwoods are also being studied. The plants and perhaps even the mushroom material will be tested in China for quality and constituent assessment. Qualified experts in the field of study will also evaluate organoleptic analysis of the plant material.

Plant It Herbs is supported in part by a second job managing the national office for United Plant Savers, (UpS) www.unitedplantsavers.org United Plant Savers is dedicated to the preservation and conservation of native medicinal plants in the US and Canada and serves as one of the most reputable organizations working to protect these valuable plant treasures.

In addition to the research work being conducted at Plant It Herbs, workshops are offered, educational support and some mail order plant sales. Please check the workshop page for updates in the fall schedule. Mail order plants will continue to be offered but not on demand as in the past. Orders will be processed and checked for availability. Most orders will be shipped in the summer or as they are propagated. Seed sales have been discontinued because they take too much time to process. Sorry.

This season the gardens are beginning to be converted using biodynamic methods. Plant It Herbs is excited to incorporate this important step into our gardening practices.

Please contact us at Plant It Herbs if you have additional questions about any of our ongoing projects.
